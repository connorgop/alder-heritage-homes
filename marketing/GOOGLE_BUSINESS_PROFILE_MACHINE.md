# Google Business Profile Machine

Goal: keep Alder Heritage Homes visible in local search without triggering unnecessary Google Business Profile post rejections.

Google's Business Profile policies are stricter and more automated than normal social media. Treat GBP posts as local trust signals, not hard-sell ads. Put stronger sales copy on the website, then use posts to point people toward useful resources.

## Why Posts Get Rejected

Common risk patterns:

- Repeating the same post text or image too often.
- Heavy sales language: "guaranteed," "best," "any problem," "instant cash," "we buy any house."
- Financial-distress pressure language: "avoid foreclosure now," "stop auction today," "debt rescue," "desperate."
- Sensitive personal-situation targeting: divorce, death, probate, liens, tax debt, foreclosure, medical, financial hardship.
- Claims that sound unverifiable or too broad.
- Images with too much text, stock-like money imagery, legal documents, checks, notices, distressed people, or before/after damage that looks shocking.
- Phone-number stuffing inside post copy. Use GBP's built-in call button instead.

## Safer Posting Rules

Use this style:

- Educational, local, calm, specific.
- One topic per post.
- No all-caps, hype, guarantees, or urgency pressure.
- Avoid naming sensitive personal problems in the first line.
- Use neutral wording: "ownership paperwork," "inherited property questions," "property tax questions," "selling options."
- Use real business photos when possible: Connor, yard signs, clean exterior shots, local Fresno/Clovis area context, desk/document photo without private info.
- Link to a matching website page instead of trying to explain everything in the GBP post.

## Image Rules

Use:

- Clean desk with generic paperwork, no private info.

- Neutral home exterior, bright daylight.

Avoid:

- Distressed people, crying faces, eviction imagery.
- Heavy text overlays.
- Reused stock photos across many posts.
- Before/after damage images that look shocking.

## If a Post Is Rejected

1. Do not keep resubmitting the same post.
2. Remove phone number from the body if included.
3. Remove hard-sell phrases and sensitive terms.
4. Swap the image for a real business/local photo.
5. Make the post educational and link to a website page.
6. Wait before trying another variation.
